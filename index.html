    • About Kareem Abdul-Jabbr


    kareem Abdul-Jabbar is an American former professional basketball player (born Ferdinand Lewis Alcindor Jr at April 16, 1947)

    played 20 seasons in the National Basketball Association (NBA) for the Milwaukee Bucks and the Los Angeles Lakers.
    During his career as a center, Abdul-Jabbar was a record six-time NBA Most Valuable Player (MVP), a record 19-time NBA All-Star, a 15-time All-NBA selection, and an 11-time NBA All-Defensive Team member.
    After his retierment he take part at some Film and TV shows
    He made his film debut in Bruce Lee's 1972 film Game of Death, in which his character Hakim fights Billy Lo (played by Lee).
